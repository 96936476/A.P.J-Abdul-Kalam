A. P. J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. Abdul Kalam, widely known as "Missile Man of India," was an inspiring scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in a small town called Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from a humble background, yet his thirst for knowledge and passion for science propelled him to the highest levels of Indian society. Kalam's early years were marked by a
love for learning, especially in subjects like mathematics and physics. He studied aerospace engineering and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he played key roles in developing India’s first satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missiles. His work on India's missile program earned him the title "Missile Man" because of his contributions to making India a self-reliant nation in missile technology. Beyond his technical achievements, Dr. Kalam was deeply admired for his values, his humility,
and his love for the younger generation. As president, he worked tirelessly to inspire students and youth, encouraging them to dream big and work hard toward their goals. His book "Wings of Fire" became a source of motivation for millions, detailing his journey from a small-town boy to a national leader. Dr. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy
lives on through his work, his teachings, and his belief in the power of education and innovation.
